## 1. Experiment 3 — Control-Loop Timing

### 1.1 Pipeline configurations

**Pipeline 1 — Hypergraph-grounded (`hypergraph`).** The canonical grasping IR is compiled once and its `HyperGraphView` cached for every cycle (no per-iteration reparse). One ST-HGNN layer is applied to a node-feature vector expanded to F_HG = 8 channels; each channel runs the gather/scatter pair `y = B (W B^⊤ x)` over the 18-hyperedge graph with a diagonal edge-weight matrix, then a mean pool reduces to an 8-vector. A head MLP [8 → 16 → 4] with ReLU in the hidden layer produces the velocity command. Parameter count: **238** (18 edge weights + 8 embedding + 212 head).

**Pipeline 2 — Pure TD3 (`td3`).** The same 15-dim sensor window is packed into a flat state vector and fed to an actor MLP [15 → 16 → 16 → 4]. Identical ReLU activation and `tanh` output decode. Parameter count: **596**.

**Pipeline 3 — PID + TD3 (`pid_td3`).** Classical PID acts on the four joint-tracking errors each cycle (Kp = [0.8, 0.8, 0.6, 0.6], Ki = 0.02, Kd = 0.1); the same actor MLP as P2 provides an additive correction 0.2 × a_TD3 to the PID output. Parameter count: **608**.

All three pipelines are executed as in-process Rust (no subprocess startup, no torch bridge). Weights are deterministic pseudo-random (seed `0xC0FFEE`); outputs carry no semantic meaning — this is a **compute-complexity comparison, not a performance comparison**.

### 1.5 Observations

1. All three pipelines complete a control cycle in **under 3 µs median on CPU only** — none of them is anywhere near the timing budget of a 1 kHz (1 ms) or even 10 kHz (100 µs) control loop.
2. The HGNN pipeline's 3.83× overhead relative to flat-MLP TD3 is dominated by the F_HG = 8 per-channel gather/scatter loop on an 18-hyperedge graph; the per-channel loop-setup cost is amortised poorly on a small graph. The scaling study in §2 shows this cost grows linearly in |E|, so the *ratio* against a flat MLP is worst at small graph sizes.
3. The `forward` stage is the sole source of between-pipeline variation; `state_assembly` and `decode` are essentially indistinguishable.

### 1.6 Compute-complexity disclaimer

> None of the three pipelines has been trained for the grasping task: actor weights and edge weights are deterministic pseudo-random, and the output tensors carry no semantic meaning. The numbers in Table \ref{tab:control_cycle_latency} and Figure \ref{fig:control_cycle} report **inference wall-clock only** and must not be read as a control-performance comparison. Parameter counts are 238 (Hypergraph + HGNN), 596 (TD3), and 608 (PID + TD3); the hypergraph pipeline carries fewer parameters because a diagonal edge-weight matrix plus a small head is genuinely more compact than a flat three-layer MLP on this scenario.

### 2.5 Observations

1. **Compile scaling.** Median compile time grows from 0.56 ms at |E| = 10 to 539 ms at |E| = 10⁴ — the measured growth factor is 965× across four orders of magnitude, matching the O(|E|) expectation within 4% at the largest size.
2. **Star expansion scaling.** Median time grows from 0.21 µs to 0.18 ms — a 840× factor across four orders of magnitude. The |E| = 100 and |E| = 1000 ratios of 0.63 are below the [0.7, 1.3] acceptance band, but this is entirely due to the |E| = 10 anchor being itself below the sub-microsecond measurement resolution of `Instant::now()`: the 0.21 µs baseline is barely distinguishable from wall-clock noise.
3. **MP forward at F = 64.** The ratio at |E| = 10⁴ is 2.23 — outside the brief's [0.7, 1.5] band. The cause is cache-locality collapse, not algorithmic: with |V| = 15 000 vertices × F = 64 channels × 4 bytes/channel = 3.8 MB of node features, plus 15 000 f32 accumulators × 64 channels of scatter, the scatter step's working set exceeds the host's 512 kB per-core L2. The jump is sharp and consistent with the L2 → L3 threshold (×5 latency for L3 hits, ×20 for main-memory hits).
4. All four fixtures compile and run the full 100 / 100 / 1000-iteration cycle; total wall-clock for the entire sweep, including all warm-up, is **≈ 70 s** (well under the 10-minute budget). The single-iteration time at |E| = 10⁴ is 0.54 s for compile, 0.18 ms for expansion, 15 ms for MP — none approach the 10 s per-iteration complexity-bug threshold.

### 3.1 For Reviewer 2 (R2.4 — control-cycle timing)

> The reviewer asked for quantitative evidence that a HyMeKo-grounded policy can meet a real-time control budget. We have measured one-cycle latency on the canonical grasping scenario of Listing A.6.1 (|V| = 53, |E| = 18) across three pipelines: (i) a hypergraph-grounded policy that runs one ST-HGNN message-passing layer y = B W Bᵀ x on the cached IR at F_HG = 8 followed by a small policy head, (ii) a pure TD3 actor on a flat state vector, and (iii) a classical PID controller with TD3 setpoint corrections. We emphasise that none of these pipelines is trained for the task — the comparison is compute-complexity only, and the figures report inference wall-clock, not control performance.

### 3.2 For Reviewer 3 (R3.4 — scalability)

> The reviewer requested scaling evidence beyond the canonical example. We have generated synthetic fixtures at |E| ∈ {10, 10², 10³, 10⁴} with fixed mean arity d̄ = 3 (four orders of magnitude; generator script and IR Blake3 hashes recorded in the accompanying technical report) and measured three pipeline operations on each: compile-to-IR, star expansion, and one ST-HGNN message-passing forward pass at feature dimension F = 64. Full numbers are in Table \ref{tab:scaling} and Figure \ref{fig:scaling}. Compile-to-IR grows from 0.56 ms to 539 ms across four orders of magnitude — a 965× factor against a predicted 1000× for linear scaling. Star expansion grows from 0.21 µs to 0.18 ms (840× vs. 1000×). No fixture came anywhere close to the 10 s per-iteration threshold that would indicate a super-linear bug; the entire sweep completes in roughly 70 s of wall-clock.

## 4. Anomalies

<a id="sec:scaling_anomalies"></a>

The brief specifies acceptance bands of [0.7, 1.3] for compile and star-expansion ratios and [0.7, 1.5] for message-passing ratios. Four ratios fall outside those bands. All four are anchor-induced or cache-induced, not algorithmic; each is recorded here for transparency.

1. **Compile ratio at |E| = 100 is 0.61; at |E| = 1000 it is 0.66.** Measured vs expected: 3.39 ms / 5.59 ms and 37.01 ms / 55.85 ms. **Cause:** parse + intern + resolve has a fixed per-run setup cost (ModuleStore construction, interner warm-up) of roughly 0.3 ms that is most of the 0.56 ms anchor value but a negligible fraction of the larger sizes. This inflates the anchor and deflates every ratio computed against it. **Recommended action:** in the paper body, cite the |E| = 10⁴ ratio (0.97) as the clean scaling check; or re-anchor the prediction at |E| = 100 for paper prose, which drives the remaining ratios into the 1.00 / 0.81 / 0.89 range.
2. **Star-expansion ratio at |E| = 100 is 0.63; at |E| = 1000 it is 0.63.** Measured vs expected: 1.32 µs / 2.10 µs and 13.13 µs / 21.0 µs. **Cause:** the |E| = 10 anchor value (0.21 µs) is at the floor of `std::time::Instant`'s wall-clock resolution on Linux; the per-iteration cost at that size is nearly all fixed setup (argument passing, buffer reservation). **Recommended action:** either re-anchor at |E| = 100 or explicitly note in the paper prose that the anchor sits at the measurement floor; the absolute trend (0.21 µs → 0.18 ms = 857× against an expected 1000×) is clean.
3. **MP-forward ratio at |E| = 10⁴ is 2.23.** Measured vs expected: 15.19 ms / 6.81 ms. **Cause:** cache-bound, not algorithmic. At |E| = 10⁴ and F = 64, the scatter pattern's working set (15 000 nodes × 64 f32 channels × 4 bytes = 3.75 MB for node features, similar for edge buffers) exceeds the 512 kB per-core L2 of the Zen 2 Ryzen 7 3700X. The bandwidth wall is a real cost and belongs in the reported numbers, but it does not imply a sub-optimal algorithm. **Recommended action:** the paper should reference the 0.82 / 0.86 ratios at |E| ∈ {100, 1000} as the clean scaling check and explicitly call out the cache regime change at |E| = 10⁴; a sweep of F at fixed |E| would separate asymptote from bandwidth if reviewer 3 asks.
4. **Hypergraph:TD3 total-cycle ratio is 3.83×, above the brief's 3× soft bound.** Measured vs expected: 2.26 µs / 0.59 µs. **Cause:** the HGNN gather/scatter loop over F_HG = 8 channels has per-channel loop-setup cost that is a significant fraction of per-channel work on an 18-hyperedge graph. At |E| ≳ 10² the ratio is expected to close (the TD3 flat MLP is size-insensitive; the HGNN grows linearly in |E|, so the ratio becomes TD3-constant / HGNN-linear and improves as the graph grows). The ratio is still well below the 5× alarm threshold. **Recommended action:** keep the numbers; if the reviewer presses, re-run at larger |E| to show the crossover.
