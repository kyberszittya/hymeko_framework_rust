\section{Purpose}

Phases 6 and 7 demonstrated that two new spectral-entropy variants —
\code{scalar_entropy_normalized} (Path B) and \code{entropy_target H*=0.5}
(Path A) — universally Pareto-dominate the original \code{scalar_entropy}
regulariser across four stress datasets (spirals, circles, MNIST plain
MLP, CapsMLP MNIST). Phase 7c extends this stress test to six additional
dataset/architecture combinations that were tested only with the
original \code{scalar_entropy} in phases 1--5:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Deep skip-connection architectures: ResMLP-20, HighwayMLP-20
  \item MNIST-shape image siblings: FashionMNIST, KMNIST
  \item Different output-class structure: EMNIST Letters (26 classes)
  \item Different input shape: SVHN ($32{\times}32$ colour)
\end{itemize}
If both new arms preserve sign and magnitude (or amplify) on these six
combinations, the universality claim covers \textbf{5 architectures
$\times$ 9 datasets} with no significant negative outliers.

Auto-fires after \texttt{Views PH7b suite finished} marker. Estimated
wall-clock: $\sim$17~hours. Output CSVs land in
\code{data/benchmarks/thesis_iv_hard_2026042?_*.csv}.

\section{Experiment cards}

\subsection{ResMLP-20 — deep residual MLP}

\textbf{Dataset / arch:} MNIST handwritten digits (60k train, 10k
test); 20 residual blocks of width 16 (\code{mnist_resnet_20}).
\textbf{Config:} 33~seeds $\times$ 5~epochs, $\lambda{=}0.1$,
reg-every-n=10, dataflow.

\textbf{Original (phase 1, \code{scalar_entropy}):}
$\Delta {=} {+}0.046$\,pp, $t{=}{+}1.76$ (marginal); replicated
$\Delta {=} {+}0.070$\,pp, $t{=}{+}1.89$.

\textbf{Expected outcome:} both new arms should keep the marginal
positive but tighten the t-statistic. Deeper architectures should
benefit \emph{more} from normalisation than plain MLPs because their
effective rank grows with depth.

\begin{Verbatim}[fontsize=\small,frame=single]
python3 python/benches/thesis_iv_hard/run_benchmark.py \
    --datasets mnist_resnet_20 \
    --arms baseline scalar_entropy_normalized \
    --seeds 33 --epochs 5 --lam 0.1 --reg-every-n 10 \
    --view dataflow --target-entropy 0.5

python3 python/benches/thesis_iv_hard/run_benchmark.py \
    --datasets mnist_resnet_20 \
    --arms baseline entropy_target \
    --seeds 33 --epochs 5 --lam 0.1 --reg-every-n 10 \
    --view dataflow --target-entropy 0.5
\end{Verbatim}

\textbf{Original:} $\Delta{=}{-}0.137$\,pp, $t{=}{-}0.55$

\subsection{EMNIST Letters — 26-class MNIST sibling}

\textbf{Dataset / arch:} 145k handwritten letters ($28{\times}28$,
26 classes, labels shifted to $0\!\ldots\!25$ from torchvision's
$1\!\ldots\!26$ convention); \code{MNISTNetSmallNClass}(26)
(\code{emnist_letters}). \textbf{Config:} 33$\times$15,
$\lambda{=}0.1$.

\textbf{Original:} $\Delta{=}{+}0.170$\,pp, $t{=}{+}1.24$
(directional, not significant). \textbf{Note:} $\sigma$ drops from
$0.941$ to $0.684$ (27\% variance reduction — strongest variance-drop
of any image dataset under \code{scalar_entropy}).

\textbf{Expected outcome:} this is the most likely big-win conversion.
Underlying variance-reduction signal is already strong; both new arms
should bring the t-stat into significance ($p < 0.05$) once
normalisation handles the larger 26-class output dimension better.

\begin{Verbatim}[fontsize=\small,frame=single]
python3 python/benches/thesis_iv_hard/run_benchmark.py \
    --datasets emnist_letters \
    --arms baseline scalar_entropy_normalized \
    --seeds 33 --epochs 15 --lam 0.1 --reg-every-n 10 \
    --view dataflow --target-entropy 0.5

python3 python/benches/thesis_iv_hard/run_benchmark.py \
    --datasets emnist_letters \
    --arms baseline entropy_target \
    --seeds 33 --epochs 15 --lam 0.1 --reg-every-n 10 \
    --view dataflow --target-entropy 0.5
\end{Verbatim}
