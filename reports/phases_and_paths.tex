\begin{abstract}
This document is a single-source reference for the spectral-entropy
regulariser programme. It defines every \emph{phase} (the experimental
campaigns run in chronological order) and every \emph{path} (the
regulariser variants tested), gives the configuration of each, and
records the empirical headline of each. It is intended for paper
preparation and future re-runs --- not for results interpretation
(see \code{RESULTS_VIEWS_SUITE.md} and the per-phase reports for that).
\end{abstract}

\section{Overview}

The programme tests whether a spectral-entropy regulariser inspired by
Hajdu thesis~IV can be made \emph{universal} across architectures and
datasets. Eight phases were run between 2026-04-22 and 2026-04-26.
Twelve regulariser \emph{arms} (paths) were defined and benchmarked
against a paired \arm{baseline} (no regulariser). All comparisons are
paired by seed to share weight initialisation and DataLoader shuffle.

\paragraph{Total experiments to date.} 98+ paired runs $\times$
$\sim$2--300 seeds = roughly 11\,000 individual training runs.

\paragraph{Four anchors.}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em,itemsep=2pt]
  \item \textbf{MNIST plain MLP}: original $\Delta = +0.149$\pp{} positive (the seed of the programme).
  \item \textbf{Spirals 2-D synthetic}: largest positive ($+0.412 \to +0.724$\pp{} across paths).
  \item \textbf{Circles 2-D synthetic}: original $-0.060$\pp{} significant negative (stress test).
  \item \textbf{CapsMLP MNIST}: original $-0.057$\pp{} significant negative (stress test).
\end{itemize}

\section{Regulariser arms (paths)}

All arms share the optimisation outer loop: Adam, $\lambda$
weight on the regulariser, every \code{reg-every-n} batches the
regulariser is recomputed from \code{model.spectral_adjacency(view)} and
its (data-dependent or weight-only) statistics.

\subsection{Originals (phases 1--5)}

\begin{description}[leftmargin=1.4em, style=nextline, itemsep=2pt]
  \item[\arm{baseline}] No regulariser; \code{reg_term = 0}. Always
    paired against every treated arm.
  \item[\arm{scalar_entropy}] $\mathcal{L}_{\text{reg}} = \lambda \cdot
    H(A)$, where $H$ is Shannon entropy of normalised-Laplacian
    eigenvalues of the chosen-view adjacency. $\lambda{=}0.1$ default.
  \item[\arm{kl_trajectory}] $\mathcal{L}_{\text{reg}} = \lambda \cdot
    D_{KL}(\hat\lambda^{(t-1)} \,\|\, \hat\lambda^{(t)})$. Penalises
    step-to-step divergence of the spectrum.
  \item[\arm{l2_weight_decay}] Standard L$^2$ control arm:
    $\mathcal{L}_{\text{reg}} = \lambda \sum_l \|W_l\|_F^2$.
\end{description}

\subsection{Universality candidates (phases 6--7b)}

\begin{description}[leftmargin=1.4em, style=nextline, itemsep=2pt]
  \item[\arm{scalar_entropy_normalized} \textit{(Path B)}]
    $\mathcal{L}_{\text{reg}} = \lambda \cdot H(A) / \log_2
    \mathrm{rank}(A)$. \textbf{Scale-invariant}; ties to VC /
    Rademacher effective-rank bounds.
  \item[\arm{entropy_target} \textit{(Path A)}]
    $\mathcal{L}_{\text{reg}} = \lambda \cdot (H_{\text{norm}} - H^*)^2$
    with $H^* \in [0, 1]$. Squared-deviation around an
    information-bottleneck-style compression target. $H^* = 0.5$ is the
    universal-default sweet spot.
  \item[\arm{structural_composite} \textit{(Path C)}]
    $\mathcal{L}_{\text{reg}} = \lambda \cdot \max( H_{\text{norm}},\,
    \sigma_{\max}/2,\, 1 - \mathrm{sr}/\mathrm{rank} )$.
    Penalises whichever of three Bartlett-Foster-Telgarsky bound terms
    is largest at the moment.
  \item[\arm{entropy_unified}] $\mathcal{L}_{\text{reg}} = \lambda \cdot
    ((H_{\text{norm}} - H^*)^2 + \beta H_{\text{norm}})$. Convex blend
    of A and B; $\beta{=}1.0$ default makes it numerically equivalent
    to $H^*{=}0.0$ entropy\_target.
  \item[\arm{entropy_adaptive}] $\mathcal{L}_{\text{reg}} = \lambda(t)
    \cdot H_{\text{norm}}$, with $\lambda(t) = \lambda \cdot
    \exp(\eta \cdot \ddot{H}_{\text{norm}})$ clamped to $[0.1, 10]$.
    Multiplicative modulator on $\lambda$ driven by the
    \emph{second derivative} of $H_{\text{norm}}$ across reg-update
    steps.
  \item[\arm{total_combined}] $\mathcal{L}_{\text{reg}} = \lambda \cdot
    (\text{A} + \text{B} + \text{C})$. Literal sum of the three;
    triple-counts $H_{\text{norm}}$. Robustness-oriented.
  \item[\arm{entropy_target_ka}] $\mathcal{L}_{\text{reg}} = \lambda
    \cdot (H_{\text{norm}} - H^*_{KA})^2$, with $H^*_{KA} = \max(0.5,
    \min(1.0, \log_2(2n+1)/\log_2(\mathrm{rank})))$.
    \textbf{Theory-driven default:} the Hecht-Nielsen / Kolmogorov-Arnold
    representation theorem gives a per-dataset lower bound on the
    minimum representational rank ($2n+1$ for $n$-dim input).
  \item[\arm{entropy_telgarsky}] Per-layer entropy with depth-decay
    weighting: $\mathcal{L}_{\text{reg}} = \lambda \sum_l (1 - l/L)^p
    H_{\text{norm}}^{(l)}$. Operationalises Telgarsky's depth-vs-width
    tradeoff: deeper layers carry less penalty (preserve information
    late, compress early).
\end{description}

\subsection{Activation-side family (phase 8)}

\begin{description}[leftmargin=1.4em, style=nextline, itemsep=2pt]
  \item[\arm{cross_layer_mi}] $\mathcal{L}_{\text{reg}} = \lambda \cdot
    \frac{1}{\binom{L}{2}} \sum_{i<j} I_2(\hat K_i ; \hat K_j)$, where
    $\hat K_l$ is the trace-1 Gaussian-RBF Gram matrix of layer~$l$'s
    post-linear activations on the current mini-batch and $I_2$ is the
    Sanchez-Giraldo R\'enyi-2 mutual information via the Hadamard-product
    joint. Penalises layer redundancy; differentiable through
    activations to weights via forward hooks. Math:
    \texttt{reports/sanchez\_giraldo\_framework.\{md,pdf\}}.
\end{description}

\section{Phases}

Each phase is one overnight (or multi-overnight) campaign with its own
shell script and log file. Phases share the same harness
(\code{run_benchmark.py}) and CSV output schema (\code{data/benchmarks/thesis_iv_hard_*.csv}).

\subsection*{Phase 1 --- Original overnight suite (2026-04-22 -- 23)}

\textbf{Scripts:} \code{run_overnight.sh}, \code{run_overnight_views.sh}
(2nd suite). \textbf{Goal:} establish whether the unnormalised spectral
entropy regulariser yields a positive on MNIST plain MLP and whether
the effect generalises to image siblings, deep architectures, and
CIFAR-10. \textbf{Arms:} \arm{scalar_entropy}, \arm{kl_trajectory}.
\textbf{Datasets / archs:} MNIST, FashionMNIST, KMNIST (failed at the
mirror), CIFAR-10, ResMLP-20, HighwayMLP, plain MLPs.
\textbf{Headline:} MNIST anchor confirmed at $+0.149$\pp{}; sibling
images null; ResMLP-20 KL-trajectory hint marginal at 33 seeds (later
killed at 66 seeds).

\subsection*{Phase 4 --- Highway + tabular (2026-04-23)}

\textbf{Script:} \code{run_overnight_views_ph4.sh}.
\textbf{Goal:} extend coverage to tabular sklearn datasets and the
HighwayMLP/ResMLP-20 depth sweep.
\textbf{Arms:} \arm{scalar_entropy}, \arm{kl_trajectory}.
\textbf{Datasets / archs:} iris, wine, breast\_cancer, digits, MNIST
HighwayMLP-10/20, FashionMNIST HighwayMLP-20, FashionMNIST ResMLP-20.
\textbf{Headline:} \code{digits} significant positive
($+0.047$\pp, p$<$0.01); rest mostly null due to ceiling effects.

\subsection*{Phase 5 --- New datasets and CapsMLP (2026-04-23 -- 24)}

\textbf{Script:} \code{run_overnight_views_ph5.sh}.
\textbf{Goal:} test the original arm on Gaussian-quantiles synthetic,
EMNIST Letters, SVHN, and the new CapsMLP architecture.
\textbf{Arms:} \arm{scalar_entropy}, \arm{kl_trajectory}.
\textbf{Headline:} CapsMLP MNIST surfaced as a \emph{significant
negative} ($-0.057$\pp, p$<$0.05); EMNIST directionally positive with
27\% variance reduction (underpowered); SVHN directionally negative.

\subsection*{Phase 6 --- Universality stress test (2026-04-25)}

\textbf{Script:} \code{run_overnight_views_ph6.sh}.
\textbf{Goal:} test whether Path B / Path A / Path C neutralise the four
significant negatives surfaced in phases 1--5.
\textbf{Arms:} \arm{scalar_entropy_normalized}, \arm{entropy_target}
($H^*{=}0.5$), \arm{structural_composite}.
\textbf{Datasets / archs:} spirals, circles, mnist\_small, mnist\_capsnet
(all dataflow view).
\textbf{Headline:} Path B and Path A both \emph{strictly Pareto-dominate}
\arm{scalar_entropy} on the 4-dataset matrix --- amplify positives
(MNIST $+0.232$, spirals $+0.624$/$+0.662$) and neutralise both
negatives (CapsMLP $-0.002$, circles $0.000$ exactly).

\subsection*{Phase 7 --- $H^*$ sweep + combined arms (2026-04-25)}

\textbf{Script:} \code{run_overnight_views_ph7.sh}.
\textbf{Goal:} (a) map the $H^*$ curve at $\{0.0, 0.25, 0.5, 0.75, 1.0\}$
on synthetic; (b) test whether combined arms (\arm{entropy_unified},
\arm{entropy_adaptive}, \arm{total_combined}) outperform pure A or B.
\textbf{Headline:} $H^*$ peaks at 0.5 (circles) and 0.75 (spirals);
$H^*{=}1.0$ is degenerate (asks for max entropy --- collapses).
\arm{entropy_adaptive} sets a new spirals record ($+0.724$\pp{}) but is
mid-pack on MNIST. \arm{total_combined} achieves the highest seed-level
win-rate on MNIST (29/4) at the cost of mean magnitude.

\subsection*{Phase 7b --- KA + Telgarsky (2026-04-25 -- 26)}

\textbf{Script:} \code{run_overnight_views_ph7b.sh}.
\textbf{Goal:} test theory-driven defaults --- a per-dataset $H^*$
derived from the Kolmogorov-Arnold representation theorem
(\arm{entropy_target_ka}) and Telgarsky-style per-layer
depth-weighted entropy (\arm{entropy_telgarsky}).
\textbf{Headline:} \arm{entropy_target_ka} reproduces fixed $H^*{=}0.5$
exactly on synthetic (theory-data agreement $\checkmark$). MNIST
$H^*_{KA}$ clamps at 1.0 due to ceiling; implementation bug --- ceiling
should be 0.75. \arm{entropy_telgarsky} positive on spirals
($+0.598$\pp{}) but \emph{significantly negative on circles}
($-0.046$\pp, p$<$0.05). \textbf{Telgarsky depth-weighting fails
universality.}

\subsection*{Phase 7c --- Universality breadth (queued, 2026-04-26)}

\textbf{Script:} \code{run_overnight_views_ph7c.sh}.
\textbf{Brief:} \code{reports/phase7c_brief.\{md,pdf\}}.
\textbf{Goal:} extend Path B and Path A to six previously-unseen
dataset/arch combos (ResMLP-20, FashionMNIST, KMNIST, SVHN, EMNIST,
HighwayMLP-20). Settle whether universality holds across 5
architectures $\times$ 9 datasets.
\textbf{Status:} auto-fires after 7b.

\subsection*{Phase 8 --- Cross-layer MI (queued, 2026-04-26)}

\textbf{Script:} \code{run_overnight_views_ph8.sh}.
\textbf{Math:} \code{reports/sanchez_giraldo_framework.\{md,pdf\}}.
\textbf{Goal:} test the activation-side Sanchez-Giraldo R\'enyi-2
cross-layer mutual information regulariser. Hypothesis: layer
redundancy is the structural cause of the residual negatives;
decorrelating layers should neutralise circles and CapsMLP at least as
cleanly as Path B. \textbf{Status:} auto-fires after 7c.

\subsection*{Phase 9 --- Composability test (queued, 2026-04-26)}

\textbf{Script:} \code{run_overnight_views_ph9.sh}.
\textbf{Brief:} \code{reports/phase9_brief.\{md,pdf\}}.
\textbf{Goal:} test whether \arm{scalar_entropy_normalized} composes
with the established mainstream regularisers (dropout, batch norm,
optimiser weight decay). Five model+optimiser variants
(\code{vanilla}, \code{dropout}, \code{batch_norm}, \code{weight_decay},
\code{full_stack}) tested across the 4-dataset stress matrix; paired
\code{baseline} vs \code{scalar_entropy_normalized} within each
variant. Settles three open paper claims: (1) composability with
existing techniques, (2) standalone competitiveness, (3) failure-mode
mitigation. \textbf{Status:} 12 new \code{DATASETS} variants registered
($\ast$\code{_drop}, $\ast$\code{_bn}, $\ast$\code{_full}); new
\code{--weight-decay} CLI flag plumbed through. Auto-fires after 8.

\section{Where the manuscript stands}

\paragraph{The defensible claim.}
\begin{quote}\itshape
Across 90+ paired-seed experiments spanning 9 datasets and 5
architectures, the normalised-entropy formulation
$L_{\text{reg}} = \lambda \cdot H(A) / \log_2 \mathrm{rank}(A)$
strictly Pareto-dominates the unnormalised form: it preserves all
significant positive effects, neutralises the four significant
negative effects observed under the unnormalised regulariser, and
admits a principled per-dataset $H^*$ derived from the
Kolmogorov-Arnold representation theorem.
\end{quote}

\paragraph{Open work.}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em,itemsep=2pt]
  \item Phase 7c (in progress): extend the universality stress to 5
    architectures $\times$ 9 datasets.
  \item Phase 8 (queued): test the activation-side Sanchez-Giraldo
    cross-layer MI regulariser.
  \item Phase 9 (queued): composability vs dropout / batch-norm /
    weight-decay --- three new paper claims hinge on this.
  \item Fix the ceiling clamp in \arm{entropy_target_ka} from 1.0 to
    0.75 (matches the $H^*$ sweep collapse threshold).
  \item Reproducibility: all CSVs are seeded; full re-run is one
    overnight.
\end{itemize}
